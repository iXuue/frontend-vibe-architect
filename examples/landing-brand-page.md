# Example: Landing Brand Page

## User Request

I need a product website. It should have strong impact and should not feel like a generic SaaS page. Visitors should understand the product is powerful in the first screen.

## Expected Routing

Read:

- `flows/intake.md`
- `output/requirement-summary.md`
- `flows/choose-track.md`
- `tracks/landing-brand.md`
- `rules/reference-adaptation.md`
- `rules/examples-are-not-requirements.md`
- `modes/dynamic-future.md`
- `modes/simple-designed.md`
- `flows/design-options.md`
- `output/design-options.md`

Primary track:

- `tracks/landing-brand.md`

Possible secondary track:

- `tracks/web-product.md` if product UI, workflow, or app proof must be shown.

Possible modes:

- `modes/dynamic-future.md` if the product needs atmosphere, motion, or a strong launch moment.
- `modes/simple-designed.md` if the brand should feel premium, editorial, restrained, or trust-led.

## Expected Requirement Interpretation

- Hard requirements:
  - Product website.
  - Strong first impression.
  - Avoid generic SaaS look.
  - First screen must communicate product strength.
- Visual intent:
  - Impactful.
  - Distinctive.
  - Credible.
- Reference examples:
  - None unless the user provides one.
- Forbidden directions:
  - Generic SaaS hero.
  - Feature-card grid as the main idea.
  - Decoration that does not explain the product.
- Assumptions:
  - The page needs a clear first-viewport thesis.
  - Product evidence should appear early.
- Open questions:
  - What is the product and what does it do?
  - What proof can be shown: product UI, demo, results, numbers, customers, workflow, examples?
  - What action should the visitor take?

## Expected Design Direction Shape

Generate 2-4 options such as:

- Product Demo First: first viewport centered on product UI or interactive proof.
- Editorial Authority: restrained but distinctive typography and strong proof sequence.
- Dynamic Launch: expressive motion and atmosphere around a clear product thesis.

Each option should explain:

- First-viewport concept.
- Signature visual element.
- Section sequence.
- CTA hierarchy.
- Proof or trust strategy.
- Risk of becoming generic.

## Expected Output Shape

First output:

- Requirement summary.
- Primary track.
- Mode candidates.
- 2-4 design options.
- Recommendation and tradeoff.
- User decision needed if the visual direction is broad.

## Notes On What Not To Do

- Do not make a generic split hero by default.
- Do not use abstract visuals without product evidence.
- Do not make every section a card.
- Do not hide what the product is below the fold.
- Do not confuse "impact" with uncontrolled animation or visual noise.
