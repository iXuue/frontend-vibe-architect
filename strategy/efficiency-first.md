# Strategy: Efficiency First

## Use When

Use when the product must support repeated work, fast task completion, dense controls, predictable workflows, or professional productivity.

## Product Fit

- SaaS tools.
- Admin systems.
- Editors and workspaces.
- Internal tools.
- Desktop-like web apps.
- Repeated mobile workflows.

## Poor Fit

- Brand-first landing pages where memorability matters more than task speed.
- Editorial or storytelling pages.
- Highly atmospheric product launches.
- Experiences where exploration is the main value.

## Design Priority

- Speed.
- Clarity.
- Predictability.
- Low friction.
- Low visual fatigue.
- Strong information hierarchy.

## Layout Tendency

- Dense but organized layout.
- Persistent navigation.
- Clear grouping.
- Tables, lists, panels, split views, tabs, filters, and toolbars.
- Stable dimensions for repeated controls.

## Visual Tendency

- Restrained palette.
- Clear contrast.
- Functional surfaces.
- Small, consistent radius.
- Minimal decoration.
- Typography optimized for scanning.

## Motion Tendency

- Subtle feedback only.
- Short transitions for state changes.
- Avoid large cinematic motion.
- Prioritize perceived responsiveness.

## Interaction Tendency

- Keyboard, pointer, and repeated-action efficiency.
- Clear hover, focus, active, disabled, and selected states.
- Undo, recovery, and error prevention when relevant.
- Avoid hidden controls.

## Suitable Tracks

- `tracks/web-product.md`
- `tracks/desktop-direction.md`
- `tracks/data-visualization.md`
- `tracks/mobile-direction.md` for repeated utility flows.

## Suitable Modes

- Usually `modes/simple-designed.md`.
- `modes/dynamic-future.md` only for controlled feedback, status, or data transitions.

## Language Interpretation

- "Simple" means efficient and clear, not empty.
- "Professional" means stable and predictable, not visually dull.
- "Dynamic" should become feedback and state clarity, not spectacle.

## Risks

- Becoming visually generic.
- Hiding brand personality too much.
- Over-compressing information.
- Ignoring onboarding or first-use clarity.

## Output Guidance

- Explain how the strategy improves task speed.
- Include density and layout decisions.
- Call out interaction states and verification needs.
- Reject visual ideas that reduce repeated-use clarity.
