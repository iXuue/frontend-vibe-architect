# Strategy: Expression First

## Use When

Use when brand personality, creative identity, emotional impression, or memorability is a central requirement.

## Product Fit

- Creative studios.
- Portfolios.
- Campaigns.
- Product launches.
- Creator tools.
- Cultural, fashion, music, art, and entertainment surfaces.

## Poor Fit

- Dense operational dashboards.
- Risk-sensitive workflows.
- Long-session professional tools where novelty becomes fatigue.
- Interfaces where users must compare data precisely.

## Design Priority

- Personality.
- Memorability.
- Distinctiveness.
- Emotional fit.
- Brand coherence.
- Controlled surprise.

## Layout Tendency

- More varied composition.
- Strong first impression.
- Expressive section rhythm.
- Intentional contrast between hero, content, proof, and action areas.
- Avoid arbitrary decoration.

## Visual Tendency

- Stronger brand system.
- More distinctive typography, imagery, color, or material.
- Visual assets matter.
- Still maintain hierarchy and readability.

## Motion Tendency

- Motion can communicate personality.
- Use signature interactions sparingly.
- Avoid constant movement in content-heavy sections.

## Interaction Tendency

- Memorable but understandable.
- Clear path to key actions.
- Expressive hover, reveal, or transition details.
- Do not hide essential controls.

## Suitable Tracks

- `tracks/landing-brand.md`
- `tracks/web-product.md` for creative tools.
- `tracks/mobile-direction.md` for consumer or creator apps.

## Suitable Modes

- `modes/dynamic-future.md` when advanced or expressive interaction fits.
- `modes/simple-designed.md` when expression should be restrained and polished.

## Language Interpretation

- "Cool" should become a specific personality direction.
- "Different" should not mean confusing.
- "High-end" should still connect to audience and offer.

## Risks

- Style overpowering purpose.
- Copying references.
- Weak usability.
- One-note visual treatment.
- Poor accessibility from expressive typography or color.

## Output Guidance

- Name the product personality.
- Tie expressive choices to audience and product goal.
- State readability and usability safeguards.
- Identify needed visual assets.
