# Strategy: Trust First

## Use When

Use when users must feel confident, safe, informed, and in control before taking action or trusting information.

## Product Fit

- Finance.
- Healthcare.
- Security.
- Legal.
- Enterprise software.
- Payments.
- Identity, permissions, or sensitive data workflows.

## Poor Fit

- Experimental brand experiences.
- Entertainment-first pages.
- Highly playful consumer campaigns.
- Interfaces where surprise is the core value.

## Design Priority

- Confidence.
- Stability.
- Transparency.
- Readability.
- Error prevention.
- Clear status and consequences.

## Layout Tendency

- Clear hierarchy.
- Predictable navigation.
- Visible summaries.
- Explicit confirmation areas.
- Strong separation between primary, secondary, and destructive actions.

## Visual Tendency

- Calm color roles.
- High readability.
- Controlled emphasis.
- Stable surfaces.
- Low visual noise.
- Avoid excessive novelty.

## Motion Tendency

- Minimal and purposeful.
- Use motion for confirmation, progress, and state changes.
- Avoid surprise, excessive delay, or decorative movement.

## Interaction Tendency

- Clear labels.
- Confirmation for risky actions.
- Error prevention and recovery.
- Persistent state visibility.
- Accessible focus and keyboard behavior.

## Suitable Tracks

- `tracks/web-product.md`
- `tracks/desktop-direction.md`
- `tracks/data-visualization.md`
- `tracks/mobile-direction.md`
- `tracks/landing-brand.md` for trust-sensitive conversion pages.

## Suitable Modes

- Usually `modes/simple-designed.md`.
- `modes/dynamic-future.md` only when advanced feeling does not reduce trust or clarity.

## Language Interpretation

- "Premium" should become quality, stability, typography, spacing, and clarity.
- "Modern" should not become experimental if the product is risk-sensitive.
- "Friendly" should not remove seriousness or safeguards.

## Risks

- Becoming too plain.
- Overusing safe colors and losing hierarchy.
- Hiding complexity instead of explaining it.
- Adding decorative effects that weaken confidence.

## Output Guidance

- State why trust is the primary strategy.
- Include readability, status, confirmation, and error-state requirements.
- Explain any rejected expressive or immersive choices.
